    What is Heartbleed?

    Heartbleed is a vulnerability in the OpenSSL protocol that allows an attacker to steal protected information. Heartbleed would allow anyone on the Internet to read system memory, which includes secret keys, passwords, and other sensitive information. It affected 17% of all SSL servers, including companies like Google, Yahoo, Netflix, Facebook and even the FBI.
